# On the Incarnation of the Word

## Author

**Athanasius of Alexandria** (c. 296–373 AD)
- Bishop of Alexandria
- Champion of Nicene orthodoxy
- Key figure at the Council of Nicaea (325 AD)

## Central Thesis

The Incarnation was necessary because:

1. **Humanity was perishing** through sin and corruption
2. **Repentance alone was insufficient** — the penalty needed payment
3. **Only the Creator could recreate** what had fallen
4. **The Word became flesh** to accomplish both: death for sin and resurrection for new life

## Key Concepts

### *Recapitulation*

What was lost in Adam is restored in Christ. The Word takes on human nature to renew it from within.

### *Deification* (*Theosis*)

"He became man that we might become divine" (*On the Incarnation*, 54:3).

### Corruption vs. Incorruption

- Humanity through sin became subject to corruption and death
- The Incarnate Word, being Life itself, could not be held by death
- His resurrection proves the defeat of death and the promise of incorruption

## Significance

Athanasius grounds the Incarnation in the very being of God — it is not an arbitrary decree but the necessary outworking of divine goodness. God creates; God redeems; God deifies.

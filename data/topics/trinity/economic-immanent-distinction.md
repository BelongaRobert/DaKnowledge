# The Economic and Immanent Trinity: A Distinction

## Overview

The distinction between the **economic Trinity** (*trinitas ad extra*) and the **immanent Trinity** (*trinitas ad intra*) represents one of the most significant theological developments in trinitarian theology.

### Economic Trinity

The *economic Trinity* refers to God's self-revelation and activity **ad extra** — toward creation. This is how God reveals Himself in salvation history:

- **The Father** as the source and origin
- **The Son** as the incarnate Word
- **The Spirit** as the one who indwells and sanctifies

### Immanent Trinity

The *immanent Trinity* refers to God's eternal being **ad intra** — in Himself, apart from creation. This is the eternal relations of origin:

- **The Father** as unoriginate
- **The Son** as eternally begotten
- **The Spirit** as eternally proceeding

## The Rule: *Opera Trinitatis Ad Extra Sunt Indivisa*

The classic Augustinian-Thomist principle states that "the external works of the Trinity are indivisible" (*opera trinitatis ad extra sunt indivisa*). All three persons work together in every external act.

## Critical Distinction

While the works are undivided, the distinctions among the persons are not merely modal but constitute the very being of God. The economic missions reveal the immanent processions without exhausting them.

## Contemporary Relevance

Karl Rahner's axiom — "The economic Trinity is the immanent Trinity, and vice versa" — has sparked significant debate about the relationship between revelation and the divine being.

## Key Questions

1. Does the economic activity of God fully reveal the immanent being of God?
2. How do we maintain real distinction without tritheism?
3. What are the implications for prayer and worship?

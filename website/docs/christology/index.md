# Christology

## The Study of Jesus Christ

Christology is the study of the person and work of Jesus Christ — the Son of God incarnate. It seeks to understand who Jesus is (His person) and what He accomplished (His work). Every other doctrine in Christian theology flows from this central truth: **God became man in Jesus Christ.**

> *"Jesus asked his disciples, 'Who do you say that I am?' Simon Peter replied, 'You are the Christ, the Son of the living God.'"*  
> — Matthew 16:15-16

---

## The Central Questions

The Church's Christological faith answers the deepest questions about Jesus:

### Who is Jesus?
Jesus is true God and true man, united in the **one divine person** of the Son. This is the doctrine of the **Hypostatic Union**.

### What did Jesus do?
Jesus revealed the Father, died for our sins, rose from the dead, and ascended into heaven. He continues to intercede for us and will come again in glory.

### Why did God become man?
> *"God became man that man might become God."*  
> — St. Athanasius, *On the Incarnation*

The Incarnation was not necessary — God could have redeemed us in countless other ways — but it was fitting. By assuming human nature, God heals it from within, raising it to share in His divine life.

## The Titles of Christ

### Christ / Messiah
**Christ** (Greek *Christos*) means **"Anointed One,"** the translation of Hebrew **Messiah**. Jesus is the fulfillment of Israel's hopes — the prophet, priest, and king anointed by God.

### Son of God
This title speaks of Jesus' divine nature and His unique, eternal relationship with the Father. It is not merely a title of honor but a confession of His divinity.

> *"The high priest asked him, 'Are you the Christ, the Son of the Blessed?' And Jesus said, 'I am.'"*  > — Mark 14:61-62

### Son of Man
Jesus' favorite self-designation, drawn from Daniel 7:13. It speaks of His true humanity, His solidarity with the human race, and His eschatological role as judge.

> *"The Son of man came not to be served but to serve, and to give his life as a ransom for many."*  > — Mark 10:45

### Lord (*Kyrios*)
In the Greek Old Testament, *Kyrios* translates the sacred name YHWH. To call Jesus "Lord" is to identify Him with the God of Israel.

> *"If you confess with your lips that Jesus is Lord and believe in your heart that God raised him from the dead, you will be saved."*  > — Romans 10:9

### Savior
Jesus' very name (Yeshua) means **"The Lord saves."** He is the Savior not merely in a political or social sense but in the ultimate sense: He saves us from sin, death, and eternal separation from God.

## The States of Christ

Traditional Christology speaks of two states in Christ's life:

### The State of Humiliation
From the Incarnation to the cross, Christ laid aside the full exercise of His divine glory (not His divinity itself) in order to identify with fallen humanity and accomplish redemption.

> *"Though he was in the form of God, he did not count equality with God a thing to be grasped, but emptied himself, taking the form of a servant."*  
> — Philippians 2:6-7

### The State of Exaltation
From the resurrection to the ascension and session at the Father's right hand, Christ is glorified in His humanity and reigns as Lord of all.

> *"God has highly exalted him and bestowed on him the name which is above every name."*  
> — Philippians 2:9

---

!!! quote "Catechism of the Catholic Church"
    **CCC 464:** *"The unique and altogether singular event of the Incarnation of the Son of God does not mean that Jesus Christ is part God and part man, nor does it mean that the Son of God took on a human person. It means that the Son of God, while remaining God, assumed a human nature. Jesus Christ is true God and true man, in the unity of his divine person."*

## Reflection

Christology is not mere speculation about a historical figure. It is the contemplation of the **living Person** who continues to act in the world through His Church and His sacraments. To study Christology is to grow in love for the One who first loved us — "not that we loved God but that he loved us and sent his Son to be the expiation for our sins" (1 John 4:10).

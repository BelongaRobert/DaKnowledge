# Spiritual Gifts

## Gifts Given for the Common Good

The Holy Spirit bestows **spiritual gifts** (charisms) upon believers for the building up of the Church and the advancement of the Gospel. These gifts are not natural talents or acquired skills but **supernatural endowments** given by grace.

> *"To each is given the manifestation of the Spirit for the common good."*  
> — 1 Corinthians 12:7

---

## The Seven Gifts of the Holy Spirit

These are the foundational gifts that perfect the virtues and dispose the soul to respond to God's grace. They are given in Baptism and increased in Confirmation.

### 1. Wisdom
The gift by which we judge all things according to God's perspective. Wisdom sees the world sub specie aeternitatis — under the aspect of eternity.

> *"The wisdom from above is first pure, then peaceable, gentle, open to reason, full of mercy and good fruits."*  
> — James 3:17

### 2. Understanding
The gift that illumines the mind to penetrate the truths of faith. It is not merely knowledge but a kind of spiritual insight that grasps the inner meaning of God's revelation.

### 3. Counsel
The gift that enables right judgment in practical matters. Counsel helps the believer discern God's will in difficult decisions.

### 4. Fortitude
The gift that strengthens the will to pursue the good despite obstacles, fear, or persecution. Fortitude is not rashness but courageous perseverance.

> *"I can do all things in him who strengthens me."*  
> — Philippians 4:13

### 5. Knowledge
The gift that enables the mind to apprehend God's plan in creation and salvation. It differs from wisdom in that knowledge looks at created things and sees God's hand in them.

### 6. Piety
The gift that fosters filial devotion to God and reverent love for others as children of God. Piety is the disposition of a heart that knows it is loved by the Father.

### 7. Fear of the Lord
The gift that produces reverence before God's majesty and holy dread of offending Him. This is not servile fear but the filial fear of a child who does not want to displease a loving father.

> *"The fear of the Lord is the beginning of wisdom."*  
> — Proverbs 9:10

## The Charisms of 1 Corinthians 12

St. Paul lists a variety of charismatic gifts given for the service of the Church:

| Gift | Description |
|------|-------------|
| **Word of Wisdom** | Supernatural insight into divine mysteries |
| **Word of Knowledge** | Supernatural understanding of facts or situations |
| **Faith** | Extraordinary confidence in God's power (miracle-working faith) |
| **Gifts of Healing** | Supernatural restoration of health |
| **Working of Miracles** | Extraordinary manifestations of divine power |
| **Prophecy** | Speaking God's word for edification, encouragement, or consolation |
| **Discerning of Spirits** | Ability to recognize the influence of good or evil spirits |
| **Tongues** | Prayer or praise in unlearned languages |
| **Interpretation of Tongues** | Understanding and explaining utterances in tongues |

These gifts are distributed according to the Spirit's will, not human merit. No one possesses all gifts, and no gift is given to everyone.

> *"All these are inspired by one and the same Spirit, who apportions to each one individually as he wills."*  
> — 1 Corinthians 12:11

## The Gifts and the Church's Hierarchy

The Church also speaks of **hierarchical gifts** given for the governance and service of the community:

| Office | Gift | Purpose |
|--------|------|---------|
| **Apostles** | Foundation-laying authority | To establish and authorize the Church |
| **Prophets** | Revealing God's word | To speak God's truth to the community |
| **Teachers** | Explaining the faith | To instruct and preserve doctrine |
| **Miracle-workers** | Demonstrating God's power | To confirm the Gospel |
| **Healers** | Restoring health | To show God's compassion |
| **Helpers** | Assisting the community | To support the Church's mission |
| **Administrators** | Governing with wisdom | To lead with order and charity |
| **Speakers in tongues** | Praying in the Spirit | To edify oneself and, when interpreted, the Church |

---

## Discernment of Charisms

Not every extraordinary manifestation is from the Holy Spirit. The Church teaches the importance of **discernment**:

- Does it build up the Church? (1 Corinthians 14:12)
- Is it in harmony with Scripture and Tradition?
- Does it produce the fruits of the Spirit — love, joy, peace?
- Is it subject to the Church's authority and pastoral guidance?

> *"Do not quench the Spirit. Do not despise prophesying. But test everything; hold fast what is good."*  
> — 1 Thessalonians 5:19-21

---

## All Gifts Serve Love

The greatest gift is not any charism but **charity** — the theological virtue of love.

> *"If I have prophetic powers, and understand all mysteries and all knowledge, and if I have all faith, so as to remove mountains, but have not love, I am nothing."*  
> — 1 Corinthians 13:2

All spiritual gifts are meant to serve the building up of the Body of Christ in love. When they become sources of pride, division, or self-seeking, they cease to serve their purpose.

# The Holy Lance: The Spear of Longinus and the Piercing of Christ's Side

!!! sacred "First-Class Relic"

 The Holy Lance is venerated as a **first-class relic**: the instrument that pierced the heart of Christ as He hung upon the cross. Together with the True Cross and the Crown of Thorns, it completes the triad of the principal instruments of the Passion.

## The Biblical Account

The piercing of Christ's side is recorded in the Gospel of John, written by the eyewitness who stood at the foot of the cross:

> *"But when they came to Jesus and saw that he was already dead, they did not break his legs. Instead, one of the soldiers pierced his side with a spear, and at once blood and water came out. He who saw this has testified so that you also may believe. His testimony is true, and he knows that he tells the truth."* — John 19:33-35

This moment, occurring after the crucifixion but before the descent from the cross, holds profound theological significance. The soldier's spear, intended merely to confirm death according to Roman practice (crurifragium, breaking the legs, was the standard method to hasten death for crucifixion victims), instead became the instrument of revelation.

### The Soldier Who Pierced

Tradition identifies the soldier as **Longinus** (Greek: Λογγῖνος), a name derived from the Greek word for "lance" (λόγχη). The apocryphal **Acts of Pilate** (also called the Gospel of Nicodemus) gives the earliest account of Longinus as a centurion stationed at the crucifixion. According to this tradition:

- Longinus was blind or had poor vision
- He pierced Christ's side to verify death
- The blood and water that flowed from the wound restored his sight
- This miracle led to his conversion and eventual martyrdom

The Eastern Orthodox Church venerates Longinus as **St. Longinus the Centurion**, celebrating his feast on October 16. The Roman Catholic Church also recognizes his martyrdom, with his commemoration on March 15 in some traditional calendars.

### Blood and Water: The Sacramental Mystery

The Fathers of the Church universally interpreted the flow of blood and water from Christ's pierced side as a profound symbol:

**The Church is born from the wound**: St. Augustine taught that as Eve was formed from Adam's side while he slept, so the Church, the New Eve, was born from Christ's side as He "slept" in death upon the cross. The blood represents the Eucharist, the water represents Baptism: the two sacraments that bring the faithful into the Church.

St. John Chrysostom preached: *"From His side came blood and water... that we might be born again through the font of Baptism, and nourished by the cup of the Eucharist."*

**The fulfillment of prophecy**: The Evangelist notes that these events occurred "so that the scripture might be fulfilled: 'None of his bones shall be broken'" (Exodus 12:46, Numbers 9:12, Psalm 34:20) and "They will look on the one whom they have pierced" (Zechariah 12:10).

## Historical Provenance

### Early Traditions

The earliest traditions regarding the Holy Lance place it in Jerusalem alongside the other Passion relics. The **Breviarius de Hierosolyma** (c. 530) mentions the spear among the sacred objects preserved in the Holy City.

By the early medieval period, the lance had been transferred to Constantinople, where it was housed in the imperial treasury. Like the Crown of Thorns and fragments of the True Cross, the lance became part of the collection of Passion relics accumulated by Byzantine emperors.

### Transfer to the West

The Holy Lance reached Western Europe through multiple channels:

**The Holy Roman Empire**: The most significant transfer occurred in the 10th century, when the German King **Henry the Fowler** (c. 876-936) received the lance from the Byzantine emperor. This spear became one of the **Imperial Regalia** (*Reichskleinodien*), the coronation treasures of the Holy Roman Empire.

The lance's political significance was immense. Medieval emperors believed the spear conferred martial power: a tradition drawn from the legend that whoever possessed the lance would be invincible. This belief persisted into the modern era, most notoriously in the esoteric theories of Nazi occultists.

**The Vatican Lance**: Another claimed Holy Lance was preserved in St. Peter's Basilica in Rome. This lance tip was given to Pope Innocent VIII in 1492 and remained in the papal collections.

**Armenian Claim**: The Armenian Apostolic Church maintains a tradition of possessing the Holy Lance, kept at the monastery of **Geghard** ("Spear Monastery") near Yerevan. This claim dates to the early Christian period and remains an important part of Armenian religious identity.

**Antioch**: During the First Crusade, a lance was reportedly discovered at Antioch by a crusader named Peter Bartholomew. This "Lance of Antioch" played a significant role in Crusader morale but was later lost or discredited.

### The Vienna Lance

The most prominent claimant to authenticity is the **Holy Lance of Vienna**, housed in the **Imperial Treasury** (*Schatzkammer*) at the Hofburg Palace. This lance features:

- A **narrow blade** of tempered iron, approximately 22 cm long
- A **gold sleeve** added in the 11th century, covering the junction of blade and shaft
- An inscription on the sleeve identifying the relic as "nail of the Lord" (possibly referring to a nail incorporated into the binding)
- A **silver wire wrapping** dating to the Carolingian period (8th-9th century)

**Scientific Analysis**: In 2003, the Vienna lance was examined by a multidisciplinary team including metallurgists and art historians. The analysis revealed:

- The blade itself dates to the **7th-8th century** (not the 1st century)
- The Carolingian wrapping is authentic to the 8th-9th century
- The gold sleeve is 11th century
- A small nail-like fragment embedded in the sleeve could not be dated definitively

These findings suggest the Vienna lance is a medieval reliquary spear rather than the original Roman weapon. However, the presence of ancient components and the documented provenance from the 10th century make it an important historical relic regardless of its connection to the crucifixion.

!!! sacred "The Power of Relics"

    The medieval belief in the Holy Lance's martial power illustrates a common pattern in relic veneration: the spiritual significance of the object becomes intertwined with miraculous or supernatural expectations. While the Church teaches that relics are channels of grace, not magical implements, popular devotion has often attributed specific powers to particular relics.

## Theological Significance

### The Opened Side

The wound in Christ's side holds profound theological meaning across Christian traditions:

**Access to the Heart of Christ**: Devotional writers, particularly in the Catholic tradition, have meditated on the opened side as the gateway to Christ's Sacred Heart. St. Bonaventure wrote: *"His side was opened, that we might enter into the secret of His heart."* The wound becomes an invitation to intimacy with the Savior.

**The Fountain of Mercy**: St. Faustina Kowalska and the Divine Mercy devotion emphasize the blood and water as symbols of God's inexhaustible mercy flowing upon humanity. The Divine Mercy image, revealed in the 20th century, depicts rays of red and white light emanating from Christ's breast: the blood and water of Calvary transformed into streams of grace.

**The Fulfillment of Passover**: The Gospel's note that Christ's legs were not broken directly connects to the Passover lamb (Exodus 12:46). St. Paul calls Christ "our Passover lamb" (1 Corinthians 5:7). The unbroken bones and the flow of blood identify Christ as the definitive sacrificial victim, whose offering takes away the sins of the world.

### Longinus: The Converted Soldier

The figure of Longinus represents one of the earliest conversions recorded in Christian tradition. The soldier who executed the command to pierce Christ becomes the first to recognize what his comrades mocked: the true identity of the crucified One.

The **Lance of Longinus** thus symbolizes:

1. **The transformation of violence**: The weapon of execution becomes an instrument of revelation
2. **Conversion through suffering**: Encounter with Christ's sacrifice opens eyes (literally, in Longinus' case) to faith
3. **The inclusivity of grace**: Even the executioner is offered salvation
4. **Military sanctity**: Longinus became the prototype of soldier saints throughout Christian history

## Liturgical and Devotional Practices

### Good Friday and the Passion

The Holy Lance is commemorated liturgically on Good Friday, when the Passion narrative is read or sung. In the solemn liturgy:

- The Gospel of John is chanted, including the piercing of the side
- The faithful venerate the cross, recalling all the instruments of the Passion
- The solemn reproaches (*Improperia*) include references to the crucifixion

In some Eastern Christian traditions, the liturgical spear (used to cut the prosphora, or communion bread) symbolizes the Holy Lance, and the warm water added to the chalice represents the water from Christ's side.

### The Lance in Christian Art

The Holy Lance appears in numerous artistic contexts:

- **Crucifixion scenes**: Held by Longinus or resting in Christ's side
- **Arma Christi**: The "arms of Christ", collections of Passion instruments frequently depicted in medieval and Renaissance art
- **Heraldry**: The spear became a symbol in various coats of arms, particularly of cities claiming relic connections
- **Reliquaries**: Elaborate containers for lance fragments, often shaped like the spear itself

### Devotional Meditations

Private devotion to the Holy Lance includes:

**The Chaplet of the Five Wounds**: A devotion focusing on Christ's five principal wounds: the hands, feet, and side. The side wound, being the largest and the source of the Church's sacraments, receives special attention.

**The Litany of the Passion**: Invocations that include reference to the piercing, such as "By Thy most precious Blood and Water flowing from Thy side, deliver us, O Jesus."

**The Golden Arrow Prayer**: A prayer dictated by Christ to Sr. Mary of St. Peter (19th century) that includes reference to the spear wound, asking that "the very tears of blood and water which flowed from Thy side" be applied to sinners.

## Competing Claims and Scholarly Assessment

### The Problem of Multiple Lances

Unlike the True Cross (which could theoretically be divided into many small fragments) or the Crown of Thorns (which retained its physical form), the Holy Lance presents a particular problem: there can logically be only one spear. The existence of multiple claimants requires careful evaluation.

**The Vienna Lance**: Best documented provenance from the 10th century, but scientific analysis dates the blade to the 7th-8th century. Likely a Carolingian reliquary incorporating older materials.

**The Vatican Lance**: A lance tip presented in 1492. Scientifically examined but not definitively authenticated. The late date of documented provenance raises questions.

**The Armenian Lance**: Claimed tradition from apostolic times, but documentation is difficult to verify independently. Significant for Armenian religious identity regardless of empirical authentication.

**The Antioch Lance**: Lost during the Crusades; historical records suggest it may have been a pious fraud or genuine secondary relic.

### The Church's Position

The Catholic Church does not definitively authenticate specific relics in most cases. Rather, local bishops approve relics for veneration based on tradition and documented custody. The Church teaches that the spiritual benefit of relic veneration does not depend on empirical proof of authenticity.

The Congregation for the Causes of Saints oversees the authentication of relics for official liturgical use, but this process focuses on documentation and custody rather than scientific testing.

## The Lance in History and Legend

### The Holy Roman Empire

The Vienna lance played a central role in the **coronation ceremonies** of the Holy Roman Empire. Emperors from Otto I to Francis II touched or carried the lance during their coronations at Aachen or Frankfurt. The spear symbolized:

- **Divine authority**: The emperor as God's representative on earth
- **Military responsibility**: Defense of Christendom
- **Continuity with Constantine**: The first Christian emperor

Napoleon deliberately refused to use the Imperial Regalia at his coronation, symbolizing the break with the Holy Roman Empire's sacred traditions.

### Modern Controversies

The Holy Lance has attracted unfortunate attention from **Nazi occultists**, who appropriated medieval legends about the spear's power. Hitler, fascinated by the lance's history, had it removed from Vienna to Nuremberg in 1938 and displayed it as part of Nazi propaganda. American forces recovered the lance in 1945 and returned it to Austria in 1946.

This dark chapter illustrates how relics, objects of Christian devotion, can be perverted for ideological purposes. The Church's teaching on relics as sacramentals (channels of grace for those who approach in faith) stands in sharp contrast to any ideology that would treat them as talismans of worldly power.

## Conclusion: The Wound That Heals

The Holy Lance, whether approached as a specific relic in Vienna, Rome, or Armenia, or as a symbol meditated upon in prayer, represents one of Christianity's most powerful paradoxes: the wound that heals, the spear that gives life, the violence that reveals love.

In the piercing of Christ's side, the last act of the crucifixion becomes the first revelation of the resurrection life that will follow. The blood and water, Eucharist and Baptism, are given to the Church before the tomb is even sealed. Grace precedes nature; the sacraments flow before the stone is rolled away.

St. Thomas Aquinas, in the hymn *Adoro Te Devote*, addresses the wound directly:

> *"I rejoice that I am not able to see,
> For faith suffices me in its place.
> O Jesus, make me wholly believe in You,
> And in You may my hope be firmly placed."*

The Holy Lance reminds the faithful that the Church was born not from power or wisdom, but from the wounded side of the crucified Savior. In that wound, physical, historical, terrible, is found the doorway to eternal life.

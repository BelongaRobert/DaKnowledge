# Anointing of the Sick

## The Sacrament of Healing and Hope

The Anointing of the Sick is the sacrament of Christ's compassion for the ill, the suffering, and the dying. It confers grace, strength, and peace — and, when God wills, bodily healing. It is not merely a "last rites" sacrament but a gift for all who face serious illness.

> *"Is any among you sick? Let him call for the elders of the church, and let them pray over him, anointing him with oil in the name of the Lord; and the prayer of faith will save the sick man, and the Lord will raise him up; and if he has committed sins, he will be forgiven."*  
> — James 5:14-15

---

## The Institution of the Sacrament

Christ healed the sick throughout His ministry — touching lepers, restoring sight to the blind, cleansing the paralyzed. He commanded His apostles to do the same (Mark 6:13). The Letter of James (5:14-15) gives the Church's earliest teaching on this sacrament.

---

## The Effects of the Sacrament

### 1. Grace and Strength
The sacrament confers grace to endure illness with faith and peace. It strengthens the soul against discouragement, despair, and anxiety.

### 2. Forgiveness of Sins
If the sick person is unable to confess, the sacrament forgives sins — even mortal sins, if the person has at least imperfect contrition.

### 3. Healing
When it is conducive to the soul's salvation, the sacrament can bring **bodily healing**. This is not guaranteed in every case — God's will is primary — but it is a real possibility.

### 4. Union with Christ's Passion
The sick person is united more deeply to the redemptive suffering of Christ. Their illness becomes a participation in the saving work of the cross (Colossians 1:24).

### 5. Preparation for Death
For those approaching death, the Anointing of the Sick prepares the soul for the final journey — giving peace, courage, and hope in the resurrection.

---

## The Matter and Form

- **Matter**: Oil of the Sick (olive oil or another plant oil, blessed by the bishop)
- **Form**: Prayer of the priest over the sick person

The anointing is done on the **forehead** and **hands** (and, in some traditions, other parts of the body). The priest prays: *"Through this holy anointing may the Lord in his love and mercy help you with the grace of the Holy Spirit. May the Lord who frees you from sin save you and raise you up."*

---

## Who Can Receive This Sacrament?

The sacrament is for those who are:
- Seriously ill (physically or mentally)
- Elderly and weakened by age
- Facing surgery
- Near death

It can be received **more than once** — whenever the illness worsens, a new serious condition arises, or recovery is followed by relapse.

---

## Viaticum: Food for the Journey

For those near death, the Eucharist received as **Viaticum** ("food for the journey") is the sacrament proper to the dying. It is the final Holy Communion of the Christian life, uniting the dying person to Christ who conquered death.

Together, the Anointing of the Sick, Reconciliation, and Viaticum form the **"Last Rites"** — the Church's care for the Christian at the threshold of eternity.

---

!!! quote "Catechism of the Catholic Church"
    **CCC 1520:** *"A particular gift of the Holy Spirit. The first grace of this sacrament is one of strengthening, peace and courage to overcome the difficulties that go with the condition of serious illness or the frailty of old age."*

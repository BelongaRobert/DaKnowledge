# Baptism

## The Door of the Church

Baptism is the **sacrament of regeneration** — the gateway to the Christian life. Through it, we are born again, cleansed from sin, incorporated into Christ, and made members of His Church. It is the **foundation** upon which all other sacraments and the entire Christian life are built.

> *"Truly, truly, I say to you, unless one is born of water and the Spirit, he cannot enter the kingdom of God."*  
> — John 3:5

---

## The Institution of Baptism

Christ instituted Baptism before His ascension:

> *"Go therefore and make disciples of all nations, baptizing them in the name of the Father and of the Son and of the Holy Spirit."*  
> — Matthew 28:19

This command — the **Great Commission** — sends the apostles (and their successors) to baptize the whole world. Baptism is not optional; it is the ordinary means by which we enter the New Covenant.

---

## The Effects of Baptism

Baptism produces a radical transformation in the soul:

### 1. Forgiveness of Sins
All sins — original sin and actual sins — are forgiven in Baptism. The soul is washed clean, restored to the state of grace that Adam and Eve possessed before the Fall.

### 2. New Birth
Baptism is a true **rebirth**. We are born into a new life — the life of grace, the life of the children of God.

> *"By one Spirit we were all baptized into one body."*  
> — 1 Corinthians 12:13

### 3. Incorporation into Christ
We are united to Christ — made members of His Body, the Church. We share in His death and resurrection, His priesthood, His prophetic mission, and His kingship.

### 4. Indwelling of the Holy Spirit
The Holy Spirit comes to dwell in the baptized person. We become temples of the Holy Spirit (1 Corinthians 6:19), receiving the theological virtues of faith, hope, and charity, and the gifts of the Spirit.

### 5. Adoption as Children of God
We become adopted sons and daughters of the Father, co-heirs with Christ (Romans 8:15-17). We can call God **"Abba, Father"** (Galatians 4:6).

### 6. Character (Seal)
Baptism imprints an **indelible spiritual mark** (character) on the soul. This mark can never be erased — not even by apostasy or excommunication. It consecrates the person for divine worship and configures them to Christ.

---

## The Matter and Form

The sacrament consists of:
- **Matter**: Water — the element of purification and life
- **Form**: The Trinitarian formula — *"I baptize you in the name of the Father, and of the Son, and of the Holy Spirit"* (Matthew 28:19)

The water can be poured, the person can be immersed, or water can be sprinkled — all are valid if the intention is to do what the Church does.

---

## Infant Baptism

The Church baptizes infants because:
- Original sin affects all, even the youngest
- The New Covenant includes children (as the Old Covenant did through circumcision)
- Parents and godparents make the profession of faith on behalf of the child
- The grace of Baptism is entirely God's gift, not dependent on the recipient's understanding

Infant Baptism is not mere ritual. It is the **supernatural birth** of a child into the life of grace — a gift far greater than any natural privilege.

---

## The Necessity of Baptism

The Church teaches that Baptism is **necessary for salvation** for those to whom the Gospel has been proclaimed and who have the possibility of requesting it. However, God's mercy is not limited to visible sacraments:

- **Baptism of blood** — martyrdom for Christ before receiving Baptism
- **Baptism of desire** — explicit or implicit desire for Baptism, coupled with repentance and charity

> *"God has bound salvation to the sacrament of Baptism, but he himself is not bound by his sacraments."*  
> — Catechism of the Catholic Church, 1257

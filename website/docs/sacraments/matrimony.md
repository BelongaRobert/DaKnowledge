# Matrimony

## The Sacrament of Covenantal Love

Holy Matrimony is the sacrament by which a man and a woman are united in a **covenant of life and love**, ordered toward the good of the spouses and the procreation and education of children. Marriage is not merely a contract but a sacred bond that mirrors the covenant between Christ and His Church.

> *"Husbands, love your wives, as Christ loved the church and gave himself up for her."*  
> — Ephesians 5:25

---

## The Institution of Marriage

Marriage was instituted by God at the very beginning of creation:

> *"Therefore a man leaves his father and his mother and cleaves to his wife, and they become one flesh."*  
> — Genesis 2:24

Christ elevated this natural institution to a **sacrament**, conferring grace upon the spouses and making their union a sign of His own union with the Church.

> *"What therefore God has joined together, let not man put asunder."*  
> — Matthew 19:6

---

## The Properties of Marriage

The Church teaches that every valid sacramental marriage has three essential properties:

### 1. Unity (Monogamy)
Marriage is between **one man and one woman**. Polygamy is contrary to the divine plan, which from the beginning established the union of two persons who become "one flesh."

### 2. Indissolubility
A valid sacramental marriage cannot be dissolved by any human power. What God has joined, no court, no culture, and no convenience can separate.

> *"Whoever divorces his wife and marries another, commits adultery against her; and if she divorces her husband and marries another, she commits adultery."*  
> — Mark 10:11-12

This indissolubility reflects Christ's eternal fidelity to His Bride, the Church. The cross is the pattern of Christian marriage: total self-gift, faithful unto death.

### 3. Openness to Children
Marriage is ordered toward the **procreation and education of children**. Every marital act must remain open to the gift of life. Contraception — the deliberate frustration of the procreative meaning of the marital act — is contrary to God's design.

---

## The Effects of the Sacrament

### 1. Bond of Grace
The spouses are united not only by human love but by a **sacramental bond** that strengthens them to live their vocation faithfully.

### 2. Grace for Holiness
Marriage is a **path to holiness**. The daily sacrifices, mutual forgiveness, and shared burdens of family life are the raw material of sanctification.

### 3. Domestic Church
The family is the **domestic Church** — the first place where faith is taught, prayer is learned, and love is practiced. Parents are the primary educators of their children in the faith.

### 4. Remedy for Concupiscence
St. Paul teaches that marriage is a remedy for the disorder of desire (1 Corinthians 7:9). The mutual gift of spouses helps them grow in purity and self-mastery.

---

## The Goods of Marriage

St. Augustine identified three goods (bona) of marriage:

| Good | Meaning |
|------|---------|
| **Fides** (Fidelity) | Exclusive and lifelong faithfulness to one another |
| **Proles** (Offspring) | Openness to children and the responsibility of raising them |
| **Sacramentum** (Sacrament) | The indissoluble sign of Christ's union with the Church |

These goods are not optional add-ons but essential to the nature of Christian marriage.

---

## Annulment vs. Divorce

The Church does not recognize **divorce** (the dissolution of a valid marriage) but does recognize **annulment** — a declaration that a valid marriage never existed from the beginning.

An annulment is not "Catholic divorce." It is a juridical determination that one or more of the essential elements of marriage was lacking at the time of consent:
- Free consent (not coerced)
- Understanding of what marriage is
- Intention to assume the obligations of marriage
- Absence of impediments (e.g., prior valid marriage)

When a marriage is declared null, the parties are free to marry in the Church. When a marriage is valid, it is indissoluble.

---

## Marriage as Vocation

Marriage is not merely a human arrangement but a **divine calling**. The spouses are called to sanctify each other, to raise children in the faith, and to be a sign of Christ's love for the world.

> *"Marriage is a school of love — a school where husband and wife learn, day by day, to lay down their lives for one another."*  
> — Pope St. John Paul II

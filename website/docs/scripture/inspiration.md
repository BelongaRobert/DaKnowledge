# Biblical Inspiration

## The Word of God in the Words of Men

Biblical inspiration is the divine act by which God guided the human authors of Scripture so that, using their own faculties and powers, they wrote what God wanted written — **no more and no less**. The Bible is truly the Word of God because God is its principal author; it is truly the words of men because human authors wrote it.

> *"All scripture is inspired by God and profitable for teaching, for reproof, for correction, and for training in righteousness."*  
> — 2 Timothy 3:16

---

## The Meaning of Inspiration

The word "inspiration" translates Greek *theopneustos* — literally "God-breathed." Just as God breathed life into Adam (Genesis 2:7), so He breathes His truth into the sacred text.

### Not Dictation
Inspiration is not **mechanical dictation**. The human authors were not passive instruments like stenographers taking dictation. They used their own language, style, culture, and personality. God worked **through** their humanity, not around it.

> *"The Bible is the Word of God in the words of men."*  
> — Pope Benedict XVI

### Not Mere Approbation
Inspiration is not merely God "approving" what human authors happened to write. God is the primary cause of Scripture — He willed it, guided it, and guaranteed its truth.

### True Authorship
The Church teaches that Scripture has **two authors**: God and the human writer. The Holy Spirit is the principal author; the human writer is the instrumental author. Both are true authors, but in different ways.

---

## The Extent of Inspiration

The Church teaches that **all parts of Scripture** are inspired — not just the religious or moral teachings but also the historical, geographical, and cultural details. However, this does not mean that Scripture is a textbook in science or history in the modern sense.

The Bible communicates truth in accordance with the literary forms and cultural conventions of its time. It is inerrant in all that the sacred authors **intended to affirm** — that is, in all that God willed to teach for the sake of salvation.

> *"The inspired books teach the truth... since God... put into the minds of the sacred writers what he wanted them to say, and no more."*  
> — Dei Verbum, 11

---

## The Role of the Holy Spirit

The Holy Spirit is the **divine author** of Scripture. He:
- **Illumined** the minds of the sacred writers to understand the truths to be communicated
- **Moved** their wills to write what God intended
- **Guided** their intellects so that their words accurately conveyed God's message
- **Preserved** the text from error in all that it intends to teach

This work of the Spirit did not override human freedom or erase human personality. The Spirit worked from within — elevating the natural faculties of the authors, not replacing them.

---

## The Human Authors

The sacred writers were real people in real historical situations:
- **Moses** — educated in Pharaoh's court, wrote in the context of Israel's covenant
- **David** — a king and poet, wrote from the experience of war, worship, and repentance
- **Isaiah** — a prophet in Jerusalem, spoke to the political crises of Judah
- **St. Matthew** — a Jewish tax collector, wrote for a Jewish-Christian audience
- **St. Luke** — a Gentile physician, wrote with careful historical research
- **St. Paul** — a Pharisee and missionary, wrote letters to specific communities with specific problems

Their humanity is not a defect in Scripture but part of its beauty. God stoops to speak through human language, human culture, and human experience — making His Word accessible to us.

---

## Inspiration and Inerrancy

The Church teaches that Scripture is **inerrant** — free from error in all that it affirms.

### What Inerrancy Means
- The Bible teaches the truth God intended to reveal for salvation
- The Bible does not deceive or mislead
- The Bible is reliable in matters of faith and morals

### What Inerrancy Does Not Mean
- The Bible is not a scientific textbook (it uses ancient cosmological language)
- The Bible is not a modern historical chronicle (it uses various literary genres)
- The Bible must be interpreted according to the genres and intentions of the sacred authors

> *"The Bible is not a collection of ancient documents for archaeologists and historians to study; it is the living Word of God, addressed to the People of God."*  
> — Pope Benedict XVI

---

## The Living Word

The Bible is not merely a historical artifact. It is the **living Word of God** — the same Spirit who inspired the text continues to speak through it today. This is why the Church reads Scripture in the liturgy, meditates on it in prayer, and submits to it in doctrine.

> *"The word of God is living and active, sharper than any two-edged sword, piercing to the division of soul and spirit, of joints and marrow, and discerning the thoughts and intentions of the heart."*  
> — Hebrews 4:12

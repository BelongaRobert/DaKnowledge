# Attributes of God

## The Divine Perfections

The attributes of God are the perfections that belong to His divine nature. They are not separate qualities added to God, but rather describe who God is in His essential being. Because God is simple (without parts or composition), each attribute is identical with God's essence — to say God is good is to say God is Being itself.

> *"Be holy, for I, the Lord your God, am holy."*  
> — Leviticus 19:2

---

## God's Transcendence and Immanence

God is both **utterly beyond** His creation and **intimately present** within it. This paradox lies at the heart of divine revelation.

### Transcendence
God is entirely distinct from creation. He is not a being within the universe but the ground of all being. He is uncaused, self-existent, and independent of anything outside Himself.

> *"To whom then will you compare me, that I should be like him? says the Holy One. Lift up your eyes on high and see: who created these?"*  
> — Isaiah 40:25-26

### Immanence
Yet God is not distant or detached. He sustains all things in existence moment by moment and draws near to His people in love.

> *"Do I not fill heaven and earth? declares the Lord."*  
> — Jeremiah 23:24

---

## The Incommunicable Attributes

These perfections belong to God alone and cannot be shared with creatures:

| Attribute | Meaning | Scriptural Basis |
|-----------|---------|------------------|
| **Aseity** | Self-existence; God depends on nothing | Exodus 3:14 — *"I AM WHO I AM"* |
| **Simplicity** | Without parts; not composed of form and matter | Deuteronomy 6:4 — One Lord |
| **Immutability** | Unchanging in nature, will, and promises | Malachi 3:6 — *"I the Lord do not change"* |
| **Eternity** | Without beginning or end; outside time | Psalm 90:2 — *"From everlasting to everlasting"* |
| **Omnipresence** | Present everywhere in His entirety | Psalm 139:7-10 |
| **Omnipotence** | All-powerful; can do all things consistent with His nature | Matthew 19:26 — *"With God all things are possible"* |
| **Omniscience** | All-knowing; knows all things actual and possible | Psalm 147:5 — *"His understanding is beyond measure"* |

---

## The Communicable Attributes

These perfections are reflected (imperfectly) in creatures made in God's image:

### Goodness
God is the supreme good, the source of all that is good. His goodness is not mere benevolence but the overflowing of His being toward creation.

> *"No one is good but God alone."*  
> — Mark 10:18

### Truth
God is truth itself. He cannot lie or deceive because His knowledge and His word are perfectly one with His being.

> *"I am the way, and the truth, and the life."*  
> — John 14:6

### Beauty
God is the perfect beauty from which all created beauty flows. ThePsalms repeatedly call the faithful to worship the beauty of the Lord.

> *"One thing have I asked of the Lord, that will I seek after: that I may dwell in the house of the Lord all the days of my life, to gaze upon the beauty of the Lord."*  
> — Psalm 27:4

### Justice
God's righteousness is the perfect harmony of His will with the moral order He has established. He rewards good and punishes evil — not arbitrarily, but in accordance with His holy nature.

> *"The Lord is righteous in all his ways."*  
> — Psalm 145:17

### Mercy
Divine mercy is not contrary to justice but its fulfillment. In Christ, mercy and justice meet: the cross satisfies justice while opening the floodgates of mercy.

> *"The Lord is merciful and gracious, slow to anger and abounding in steadfast love."*  
> — Psalm 103:8

### Love
Love is the very name of God (1 John 4:8). Not merely an emotion or action, love is the eternal act of the Trinity — the Father loving the Son in the Spirit, and the Son returning that love to the Father.

> *"God is love, and he who abides in love abides in God, and God abides in him."*  
> — 1 John 4:16

---

## The Divine Will

God's will is perfectly free, perfectly good, and perfectly efficacious. He does not will arbitrarily; He wills according to His wisdom and goodness. Every act of God is simultaneously an expression of justice, mercy, and love.

> *"Our God is in the heavens; he does whatever he pleases."*  
> — Psalm 115:3

This sovereignty does not negate human freedom but grounds it. God wills that we freely choose Him, and His grace makes that freedom possible.

---

!!! quote "St. Thomas Aquinas"
    *"God is His own existence and His own essence. In Him, existence and essence are not distinct, as they are in creatures. Therefore, God's attributes are not accidents added to His substance, but are identical with His very being."*
    — Summa Theologica, I, q. 3, a. 3

---

## Reflection

To contemplate the attributes of God is to be drawn into worship. We do not study God as a distant object but as the living One who invites us into communion. The knowledge of God's perfections should produce not pride but humility, not fear alone but love.

> *"The fear of the Lord is the beginning of wisdom, and the knowledge of the Holy One is insight."*  
> — Proverbs 9:10

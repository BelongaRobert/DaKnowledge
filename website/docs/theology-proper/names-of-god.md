# Names of God

## How God Reveals Himself

In Scripture, God makes Himself known through names. Each name is not merely a label but a revelation of God's nature, character, and relationship with His people. To know God's name is to know something of who He is.

> *"The name of the Lord is a strong tower; the righteous man runs into it and is safe."*  
> — Proverbs 18:10

---

## Elohim

The most common name for God in the Old Testament, used over 2,500 times. **Elohim** is a plural form that can refer to the true God, false gods, or even human judges. When used of the true God, the plural form hints at the mystery of the Trinity while the singular verb usage affirms monotheism.

> *"In the beginning, Elohim created the heavens and the earth."*  
> — Genesis 1:1

Elohim speaks of God as **Creator** — the all-powerful one who brings order out of chaos, who speaks and it is done.

---

## Yahweh (YHWH)

The personal, covenant name of God revealed to Moses at the burning bush. Traditionally translated as **"the Lord"** in English Bibles (with small caps), and rendered as **Jehovah** in older translations.

> *"God said to Moses, 'I AM WHO I AM.' And he said, 'Say this to the people of Israel: I AM has sent me to you.' ... This is my name forever, and thus I am to be remembered throughout all generations."*  
> — Exodus 3:14-15

The name **Yahweh** derives from the Hebrew verb *hayah* (to be) and speaks of God's self-existence, His eternal presence, and His faithfulness to His promises. He is the God who **is** — not a God of the past or future only, but the ever-present "I AM."

---

## El Shaddai

Traditionally translated **"God Almighty,"** though the precise meaning is debated. Some scholars suggest it means "God of the Mountain" or "God the All-Sufficient One."

> *"I am God Almighty [El Shaddai]; walk before me, and be blameless."*  
> — Genesis 17:1

El Shaddai is the name by which God revealed Himself to the patriarchs — Abraham, Isaac, and Jacob. It speaks of divine power sufficiency for every need.

---

## Adonai

Meaning **"Lord"** or **"Master,"** this name emphasizes God's sovereign authority and ownership. To call God Adonai is to acknowledge that we are His servants and stewards.

> *"O Lord God [Adonai Yahweh], you have only begun to show your servant your greatness and your mighty hand."*  
> — Deuteronomy 3:24

In later Jewish tradition, out of reverence for the sacred name Yahweh, Jews began pronouncing the written text YHWH as **Adonai** when reading Scripture aloud.

---

## El Elyon

**"God Most High"** — the name that speaks of God's supreme sovereignty over all powers, nations, and authorities.

> *"Melchizedek king of Salem brought out bread and wine. He was priest of God Most High [El Elyon]."*  
> — Genesis 14:18

Jesus is called a priest "according to the order of Melchizedek" (Psalm 110:4, Hebrews 5:6), connecting this ancient name to Christ's eternal priesthood.

---

## El Roi

**"The God who sees me"** — revealed to Hagar in the wilderness when she fled from Sarah.

> *"She called the name of the Lord who spoke to her, 'You are a God of seeing,' for she said, 'Have I really seen God and remained alive after seeing him?'"*  
> — Genesis 16:13

This name reveals God's personal care. He is not a distant deity but the One who sees the outcast, the suffering, and the forgotten.

---

## Jehovah-Jireh

**"The Lord will provide"** — the name Abraham gave to the mountain where God provided a ram in place of Isaac.

> *"Abraham called the name of that place The Lord will provide [Yahweh Yireh], as it is said to this day, 'On the mount of the Lord it shall be provided.'"*  
> — Genesis 22:14

This name is a prophecy of Calvary, where God would provide His own Son as the Lamb who takes away the sin of the world.

---

## Abba

The Aramaic word for **"Father"** — intimate, familial, the language of the home. Jesus teaches His disciples to address God as **"Our Father"** (Matthew 6:9), and the Spirit enables us to cry **"Abba! Father!"** (Romans 8:15, Galatians 4:6).

> *"Abba, Father, all things are possible to you."*  
> — Mark 14:36 (Jesus in Gethsemane)

This is the revolutionary revelation of the New Testament: the transcendent, holy God of Israel is also the loving Father who adopts us as His children through Christ.

---

## The Name of Jesus

The name above every name, given by the angel before His birth: **"You shall call his name Jesus, for he will save his people from their sins"** (Matthew 1:21).

> *"At the name of Jesus every knee should bow, in heaven and on earth and under the earth, and every tongue confess that Jesus Christ is Lord, to the glory of God the Father."*  
> — Philippians 2:10-11

To invoke the name of Jesus is to invoke His saving power. The Church has always recognized the power of the holy name — in prayer, in exorcism, in healing, and in blessing.

---

## The Sacred Tetragrammaton

The four Hebrew letters **YHWH** (יהוה) constitute the sacred name revealed to Moses. Out of profound reverence, Jews stopped pronouncing this name aloud, substituting Adonai (Lord) or HaShem (the Name).

The Catholic Church honors this reverence while recognizing that the fullness of God's name is revealed in Jesus Christ:

> *"I have manifested thy name to the men whom thou gavest me."*  
> — John 17:6

Jesus manifests the Father's name not by speaking the syllables YHWH but by revealing the Father's heart — love, mercy, justice, and truth.

## Reflection

Every name of God is an invitation. Elohim calls us to trust the Creator. Yahweh calls us to covenant fidelity. Abba calls us into the intimate embrace of divine sonship. The names of God are not dry theological data — they are the living revelation of the One who seeks us, saves us, and sanctifies us.

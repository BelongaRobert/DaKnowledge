# God the Father

## The Source and Origin

God the Father is the **first person of the Holy Trinity**, the unoriginate origin of all things. He is the principle without principle — not caused by any other, not begotten, not proceeding. From Him, the Son is eternally begotten, and the Holy Spirit eternally proceeds.

> *"Blessed be God the Father of our Lord Jesus Christ, who has blessed us in Christ with every spiritual blessing in the heavenly places."*  
> — Ephesians 1:3

---

## The Father in the Economy of Salvation

In the plan of salvation (the **economy**), the Father is the source of all grace. He creates through the Word and perfects through the Spirit. He sends the Son into the world. He adopts us as His children through Baptism.

> *"For God so loved the world that he gave his only Son, that whoever believes in him should not perish but have eternal life."*  
> — John 3:16

This verse reveals the heart of the Father: **love**. Not wrath, not distant sovereignty, but self-giving love that sends the Son to redeem humanity.

### The Father's Actions in Salvation History

| Action | Scripture | Meaning |
|--------|-----------|---------|
| **Creation** | Genesis 1:1 | The Father speaks the world into being through the Word |
| **Election** | Ephesians 1:4-5 | He chooses us in Christ before the foundation of the world |
| **Sending** | John 3:16, Galatians 4:4 | He sends the Son at the fullness of time |
| **Adoption** | Romans 8:15-17 | He makes us co-heirs with Christ |
| **Glorification** | John 17:24 | He wills that we behold the glory of the Son |

---

## The Father as Creator

The Father is the source of all that exists. Creation is not a necessary emanation from God (as some philosophies teach) but a **free act of love**. God did not need creation; He willed it out of the abundance of His goodness.

> *"In the beginning God created the heavens and the earth."*  > — Genesis 1:1

Creation is **ex nihilo** (out of nothing). God did not fashion the world from pre-existing matter but spoke it into being by His word. This reveals the Father's absolute power and sovereignty.

---

## The Father in the Teaching of Jesus

Jesus reveals the Father as no one before Him ever had. He calls God **"Abba"** — a term of intimate familiarity, the word a child uses for their father. At the same time, He teaches us to approach God with holy reverence: **"Our Father, who art in heaven, hallowed be thy name."**

> *"All things have been delivered to me by my Father; and no one knows the Son except the Father, and no one knows the Father except the Son and any one to whom the Son chooses to reveal him."*  
> — Matthew 11:27

Jesus is the **only-begotten Son** — unique in His eternal generation from the Father. Yet through grace, we are adopted as sons and daughters and given the Spirit of sonship.

---

## The Fatherhood of God

God's fatherhood is not metaphorical. It is real, though analogical — God is Father in a way that infinitely transcends human fatherhood while also fulfilling its deepest meaning.

### The Father Provides
> *"Look at the birds of the air: they neither sow nor reap nor gather into barns, and yet your heavenly Father feeds them."*  
> — Matthew 6:26

### The Father Disciplines
> *"The Lord disciplines him whom he loves, and chastises every son whom he receives."*  
> — Hebrews 12:6

### The Father Forgives
> *"While he was still a long way off, his father saw him and was moved with compassion, and ran and embraced him and kissed him."*  
> — Luke 15:20 (the Prodigal Son)

---

!!! quote "Catechism of the Catholic Church"
    **CCC 239:** *"By calling God 'Father,' the language of faith indicates two main things: that God is the first origin of everything and transcendent authority; and that he is at the same time goodness and loving care for all his children."*

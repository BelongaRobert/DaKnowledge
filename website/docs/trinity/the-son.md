# God the Son

## The Eternal Word

The Son is the **second person of the Holy Trinity**, eternally begotten of the Father. He is the divine Word through whom all things were made, the perfect image of the invisible God, and the Savior of the world.

> *"In the beginning was the Word, and the Word was with God, and the Word was God. He was in the beginning with God; all things were made through him, and without him was not anything made that was made."*  
> — John 1:1-3

---

## Eternal Generation

The Son is **begotten, not made**. His generation is eternal — there was never a time when the Son was not. This distinguishes Him from creation (which had a beginning) and shows that He shares the same divine nature as the Father.

> *"We believe in one Lord, Jesus Christ, the only Son of God, eternally begotten of the Father, God from God, Light from Light, true God from true God, begotten, not made, one in Being with the Father."*  
> — Nicene Creed

To be "begotten" in this sense does not imply a beginning in time but a mode of origin within the eternal life of God. The Father is the source; the Son is the perfect expression of that source.

---

## The Incarnation

The Son, who is eternally God, became man in Jesus of Nazareth. This is the **Incarnation** — the central mystery of the Christian faith.

> *"And the Word became flesh and dwelt among us, full of grace and truth; we have beheld his glory, glory as of the only Son from the Father."*  > — John 1:14

The Son assumed a true human nature — body, soul, intellect, will — without ceasing to be God. In Jesus Christ, there are **two natures** (divine and human) united in **one divine person**.

For more on the union of natures, see [Christology: Hypostatic Union](../christology/hypostatic-union.md).

## The Son's Mission in Salvation

The Son came to reveal the Father and to accomplish the work of redemption.

| Aspect | Scripture | Meaning |
|--------|-----------|---------|
| **Revelation** | John 1:18 | "No one has ever seen God; the only Son, who is in the bosom of the Father, he has made him known." |
| **Redemption** | Mark 10:45 | "The Son of man came not to be served but to serve, and to give his life as a ransom for many." |
| **Reconciliation** | Colossians 1:20 | "Through him God was pleased to reconcile to himself all things, whether on earth or in heaven." |
| **New Creation** | 2 Corinthians 5:17 | "If anyone is in Christ, he is a new creation." |

---

## The Son in the Gospels

The Gospel of **Matthew** presents Jesus as the Son of David, the Messiah-King who fulfills the Old Testament.  
The Gospel of **Mark** presents Jesus as the Suffering Servant who gives His life as a ransom.  
The Gospel of **Luke** presents Jesus as the compassionate Savior who seeks the lost and the outcast.  
The Gospel of **John** presents Jesus as the eternal Word, the divine Son equal with the Father.

> *"I and the Father are one."*  > — John 10:30

This statement of unity does not erase the distinction of persons — Jesus and the Father are not the same person — but affirms that they share one divine nature and one divine will.

---

## Sonship and Our Adoption

The Son's unique, eternal sonship is the pattern for our adopted sonship. By grace, we are made "partakers of the divine nature" (2 Peter 1:4) and receive the Spirit of adoption (Romans 8:15).

> *"See what love the Father has given us, that we should be called children of God; and so we are."*  > — 1 John 3:1

Our sonship is real but not natural — it is supernatural, given by grace, and perfected in glory.
